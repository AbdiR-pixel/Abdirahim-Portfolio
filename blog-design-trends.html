5 Design Trends That Will Define 2024

Abdirahim Mahamoud
Digital Designer
Apr 30, 2025 5 min read

As we move into an exciting new phase of digital design, 2024 brings with it a wave of creative possibilities. From immersive 3D experiences to the resurgence of brutalism, this year's trends push the boundaries of what's possible in web, UI/UX, and branding design. Here are five trends you need to know.

1. Immersive 3D and Motion Design

Three-dimensional design is no longer reserved for gaming or film. Thanks to tools like Spline, Blender, and advances in WebGL, designers are now integrating interactive 3D elements directly into websites and apps. These elements create depth, enhance storytelling, and make users feel truly part of the experience.

Whether it's a rotating product model, a parallax 3D background, or an interactive animation, motion design elevates static interfaces into living, breathing experiences. Micro-interactions and scroll-triggered animations will continue to dominate in 2024.

How to apply it:

  • Use Spline or Three.js to add lightweight 3D elements to hero sections
  • Apply scroll-triggered animations to reveal content progressively
  • Pair 3D visuals with minimalist layouts to avoid visual overload

2. Bento Grid Layouts

Inspired by Apple's marketing pages and tech product launches, bento grid layouts have taken the design world by storm. These grid-based compositions organize content into modular tiles of varying sizes, creating a harmonious yet dynamic visual rhythm.

Bento grids are particularly powerful for dashboards, portfolio showcases, and feature highlight sections. They allow designers to present dense information in a clean, digestible format.

"Design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works." — Steve Jobs

3. Dark Mode Elegance

Dark mode has evolved from a simple color preference to an entire design philosophy. In 2024, dark-themed interfaces are being crafted with careful attention to contrast, hierarchy, and atmospheric depth. Designers are exploring deep blacks, rich charcoals, and subtle dark gradients to create premium, sophisticated aesthetics.

Paired with accent colors like amber, electric blue, or neon green, dark mode interfaces evoke a sense of exclusivity and modern refinement.

4. AI-Generated Visual Aesthetics

Artificial intelligence has opened up new creative frontiers. AI-generated imagery and design elements are finding their place in branding, editorial design, and social media content. Rather than replacing designers, AI tools act as creative collaborators that can rapidly prototype concepts and explore visual directions.

Tools like Midjourney, DALL·E, and Adobe Firefly are being used to generate mood boards, concept art, and even production-ready assets that would have taken hours to create manually.

5. Typographic Maximalism

Typography is taking center stage. Gone are the days of timid, small-scale type. In 2024, we're seeing designers use massive, expressive typography as the primary design element — letting words become the visual. Oversized serif fonts, experimental display typefaces, and kinetic (animated) text are all having a major moment.

This approach works beautifully in hero sections, editorial layouts, and brand identities where you want to make a bold statement.

Tips for typographic maximalism:

  • Pair one expressive display typeface with a clean sans-serif for body text
  • Use negative space strategically to let type breathe
  • Experiment with font weights, tracking, and scale contrast
  • Consider animated text for landing pages and hero sections

These five trends reflect a design landscape that values both technological innovation and emotional resonance. As you approach your next project, consider how you can incorporate one or more of these directions to create something truly memorable.
